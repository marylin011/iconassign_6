What could New York City pay for if the Yankees paid taxes?

By Mary Steffenhagen
October 10, 2020

The New York Yankees team pays one dollar per year in rent to the city for Yankee Stadium and pays no property taxes on the stadium itself. Instead, it makes Payments in Lieu of Taxes (PILOTS) to the city towards its debt, rather than paying taxes that would go back into the surrounding neighborhood—which happens to be the country’s poorest congressional district.

New York City and the federal government subsidized the stadium’s construction to the tune of $1.2 billion, over half the full cost of the stadium. But the Brookings Institute found little evidence that government-subsidized sports stadiums provide “spillover gains to the local economy.” Stadiums don’t appear to have a positive impact on “local economic development, income growth, or job creation,” their report says.

Yankee Stadium has cost the federal government $810 million in lost tax revenue since 2006, according to the Brookings Institute. To put that into perspective, let's look at New York City's 2021 budget figures. What could $810 million pay for?
